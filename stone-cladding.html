FAQ

Commonly Asked Questions

Stone cladding is a method of covering the exterior or interior walls of a building with a thin layer of stone or stone-like material to create a natural stone finish.

Stone cladding provides a range of benefits, including improved aesthetics, increased durability and weather resistance, reduced maintenance requirements, and enhanced insulation and energy efficiency.

Stone cladding can be installed on a range of buildings, including residential homes, commercial buildings, and public facilities such as schools and hospitals.

There are various types of stone cladding available, including natural stone cladding, manufactured stone cladding, brick cladding, concrete cladding, and metal cladding.

The installation time for stone cladding depends on the size and complexity of the project. Generally, it takes anywhere from a few days to several weeks to complete a stone cladding installation.

Stone cladding can be installed on most types of walls, including masonry, concrete, and timber-framed walls. However, it is essential to ensure that the wall is structurally sound and capable of supporting the weight of the cladding.

The cost of stone cladding depends on various factors, including the type of stone, the size and complexity of the project, and the cost of labor in your area. However, it is generally considered to be a cost-effective option compared to natural stone construction.

Stone cladding is relatively low maintenance, requiring occasional cleaning and inspections to ensure that it remains in good condition. It is also essential to inspect the cladding regularly to identify any issues that may require repairs or maintenance.
